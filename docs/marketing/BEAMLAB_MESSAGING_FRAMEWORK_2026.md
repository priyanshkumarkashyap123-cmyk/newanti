## MESSAGING DO's & DON'Ts

### ✅ DO

- **Lead with geotechnical + bridge differentiators** — This is our moat; don't bury it
- **Use regional language** — "Indian engineers built this," "AASHTO LRFD native," "ISO 1993 integrated"
- **Show, don't tell** — Videos, case studies, screenshots beat claims
- **Be technical** — Our audience is engineers; they appreciate precision
- **Acknowledge gaps honestly** — "We don't have native mobile yet; here's our roadmap"
- **Reference design code clauses** — "IS 456 Cl. 40.1 validation" builds trust
- **Emphasize validation** — "NAFEMS-tested," "textbook-verified," "peer-reviewed"

### ❌ DON'T

- **Copy SkyCiv's messaging** ("Easy to use," "No installation")—we lose on UX, so don't play that game
- **Oversell AI** — "AI does everything" is hype; position as "AI accelerates, humans verify"
- **Ignore mobile gap** — Don't pretend we have it; be transparent about roadmap
- **Use vague language** — "Powerful," "Advanced," "Best-in-class" are meaningless without proof
- **Underestimate non-India markets** — US/EU expansion opportunity is real (geotechnical + bridge design is valuable globally)
- **Discount SkyCiv competitor** — Acknowledge they're good at SMB UX; we're good at technical depth
