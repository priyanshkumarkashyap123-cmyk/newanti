Test 1: Triangular Load on Cantilever Beam

Problem Setup

Cantilever beam (fixed at left, free at right) with triangular load:

  • Length: L = 10 m
  • Load: w(x) = w₀·x/L, where w₀ = 10 kN/m
  • Material: E = 200 GPa
  • Section: I = 0.0001 m⁴
Theoretical Solution (Beam Theory):
Maximum deflection at tip: δ = 11wL⁴/(120EI)
For triangular load: δ = (11 × 10 × 10⁴) / (120 × 200×10⁹ × 0.0001)
δ = 1,100,000 / 2,400,000,000 = 0.000458 m = 0.458 mm

Test 2: Trapezoidal Load Decomposition

Problem Setup

Simply supported beam with trapezoidal load:

  • Length: L = 8 m
  • Load: w₁ = 5 kN/m (left), w₂ = 15 kN/m (right)
  • Material: E = 200 GPa
  • Section: I = 0.0002 m⁴
Load Decomposition:
UDL component: w_uniform = 5 kN/m
Triangular component: w_tri = (15-5) = 10 kN/m
Total load: W = (w₁ + w₂) × L / 2 = (5+15) × 8 / 2 = 80 kN
Reactions: R₁ + R₂ = 80 kN

Test 3: P-Delta Second-Order Effects

Problem Setup

Column with lateral load and axial compression (P-Delta effects):

  • Height: H = 6 m
  • Axial load: P = 1000 kN (compression)
  • Lateral load: F = 10 kN at top
  • Material: E = 200 GPa
  • Section: I = 0.0005 m⁴, A = 0.01 m²
First-Order Deflection:
δ₁ = FH³/(3EI) = (10×10³ × 6³)/(3 × 200×10⁹ × 0.0005)
δ₁ = 2,160,000 / 300,000,000 = 0.0072 m = 7.2 mm

Amplification Factor:
λ = 1 / (1 - P/P_E), where P_E = π²EI/H²
P_E = π² × 200×10⁹ × 0.0005 / 36 = 27,434 kN
λ = 1 / (1 - 1000/27434) = 1.038
δ_P-Delta ≈ 1.038 × 7.2 = 7.47 mm

Test 4: Euler Buckling Analysis

Problem Setup

Pin-ended column buckling load:

  • Length: L = 4 m
  • Material: E = 200 GPa
  • Section: I = 0.0004 m⁴
Euler Buckling Formula:
P_cr = π²EI / L²
P_cr = π² × 200×10⁹ × 0.0004 / 16
P_cr = 789,568,000 / 16 = 49,348 kN
